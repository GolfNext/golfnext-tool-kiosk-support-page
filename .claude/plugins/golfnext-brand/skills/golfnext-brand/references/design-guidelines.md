# GolfNext Design Guidelines

> Based on the Brand & Design Manual (119 pages, October 2025).
> Use this reference for all visual design decisions.

## 1. Visual Identity -- Logo

### Logo Description
Inspired by golf ball dimples and hexagonal geometry. Symbolizes precision, structure, and innovation. Clean, sleek, contemporary design.

### Logo Composition
- **Brandmark** (hexagonal icon) + **Logotype** (wordmark "GOLFNEXT")
- Available in: Horizontal, Vertical, Logotype only, Brandmark only

### Logo Colors
Can be rendered in: dark green, light green, clay, black, and white.

### Logo Clearspace
- Clearspace = 2x the spacing between brandmark and logotype
- **Minimum size print:** 25mm
- **Minimum size digital:** 100px

### Brandmark Standalone
- Used where space is limited (apps, mobile, small merchandise)
- **Minimum size print:** 12mm
- **Minimum size digital:** 45px

### Logo Misuse (DO NOT)
- Apply gradient
- Rotate
- Change color outside palette
- Outline or add keyline
- Change typeface or recreate brandmark
- Distort or wrap
- Use drop shadows or effects
- Change transparency
- Crop the logo

## 2. Typography

### Primary Typeface: Sora
Used for headings and key communications.
Weights: Light, Regular, SemiBold, Bold

### Secondary Typeface: Manrope
Used for body text and longer content. Clean, humanist design.
Weights: Light, Regular, Medium, Bold

### Replacement Typefaces
- **Digital (newsletters, online docs):** Inter
- **Office documents:** Arial

### Typography Hierarchy

| Element | Font | Weight | Size | Leading |
|---------|------|--------|------|---------|
| Header 1 | Sora | SemiBold | 120pt | 120pt |
| Header 2 | Sora | Light | 88pt | 88pt |
| Header 3 | Sora | SemiBold | 54pt | 58pt |
| Body Copy | Manrope | Light | 21pt | 32pt |
| Call-to-Action | Sora | SemiBold | 21pt | 32pt |

### Typography Misuse (DO NOT)
- Use fonts other than Sora or Manrope (or approved fallbacks)
- Change typography color outside palette
- Rotate on extreme angles
- Outline or add keyline
- Add unwanted embellishments
- Create challenging/chaotic layouts
- Use drop shadows or effects
- Change transparency
- Rearrange hierarchy

### Color Usage Rules

- Use black/white-only color combinations
- Pick colors outside the palette
- Use high-contrast combinations that impair readability
- Change typography color outside palette
- Combine sand/beige tones with lime tones

## 4. Photography

### Principles
1. **Authenticity** -- Real experience, no artificial staging
2. **Atmosphere** -- Natural light, especially sunset tones, warm immersive mood
3. **Motion** -- Players in action, energy and precision
4. **Detail** -- Textures, equipment, course elements

### Photo Categories
- **Player Moments** -- Golfers swinging, practicing, relaxing. Dual-tone in #B0D169 and #053E3F
- **Detail Spotlight** -- Textures, equipment, course details. Subtle dual-tone overlays
- **Course Showcase** -- Wide views of courses and driving ranges

### Photo Treatments
- Dual-tone treatments using: **#B0D169** (lime) and **#053E3F** (midnight green)
- Natural warm sunset colors also used

### Photo Shapes on Website
- Supporting shapes (standalone or with device mockups)
- Rectangular with rounded edges for thumbnails, video previews, blog posts

## 5. Brand Elements

### Iconography
- Simple, sharp, consistent
- Modern yet timeless
- Clean lines, balanced forms
- 24x24 grid
- Icon set includes: Golf Buggy, Augmented Reality, Golf, Gift Box, Outdoor Table, Restaurant, Golf Practice, Safe, Golf Ball, Headset
- Complex icons: with background shapes, richer details (Driving Range Op, Financial Reports, Phone, Player Reports, Address, E-mail, Hotline, etc.)

### Style Elements -- Linear
- Line graphics inspired by golf course layouts
- Can be used independently or combined with photos
- Color combos within GolfNext palette
- Reduced transparency (40%-70%) may be applied

### Style Elements -- Shape
- Inspired by natural curves and contours of golf courses
- Fluid, adaptable for digital and print
- Photos can be framed within shape graphics

### Graphic Usage Rules
- Linear graphics + Text: Place around text or along edges, avoid beneath body text
- Shape graphics + Text: Beneath text areas without icons
- Graphics + Photo: Add linear elements or signature shapes to photos
- Keep combinations clean, not too busy

## 6. Applications Summary

### Print Materials
- Stationery (letterheads, business cards in dark green + lime + white)
- Posters, Roll Ups, Billboards
- Brochures (oat/clay palette with dark green accents)
- Pricelists/Proposals

### Digital Media
- Website: Dark green hero, brandmark, featured projects, sustainability section
- Mobile: Fully responsive
- Email Signatures: Uncut Sans font (SemiBold 12px for name, Book 12px for details)
- Newsletter: Clay, oat, and green palette
- Social Media: Templates with brand colors, typography, logos
- Digital Banners: Horizontal (16:9) and Vertical (9:16)

### Promotional Materials
Sport bags, textile (tote bags in dark green + lime), golf balls, ID card badges, hats, water bottles, pencils (min logo: 45mm)

### Signage & Interior
Projecting signs, round signboards, wayfinding (window stickers with room name/number)

### Environmental Design
Agency front, environmental graphics ("Innovate, Sustain, Inspire"), flags (lime background + green logo)

### Reports
Annual reports in seafoam green and midnight green with white text

### Events
Stand design with brand colors, conference presentations

### App Design
Loading screen: logo on vibrant green background. Interface: user-friendly header, body text, illustration, CTA

### Vehicle Design
Van and hatchback wraps in Sacramento green with logo, slogan, and website
