# GolfNext Tone of Voice

> Brand Communication Guidelines | Version 1.0 | February 2026

## 1. Who We Are

GolfNext is a golf technology company founded in Denmark in 2012. We build self-service platforms, driving range payment systems, and digital infrastructure for golf clubs and venues across 18 countries. We serve 400+ venues and 350,000+ active users.

What makes us different: we're golfers. We didn't come from the tech industry looking for a market -- we came from the golf world and built the technology it needed. Every product we create is shaped by real conversations with club owners, managers, and players.

Our core belief: golf clubs deserve open, flexible technology that adapts to them -- never the other way around.

## 2. Our Voice in Three Words

**Credible.** We know what we're talking about because we live it. With 12+ years of experience and hundreds of venues running on our platform, we've earned the right to speak with authority. But we never flaunt it -- we let results do the talking.

**Knowledgeable.** We understand golf operations from the inside out. We know the difference between a club manager's Tuesday headache and a board chairman's annual budget worry. That depth of understanding comes through in everything we write.

**Human.** Technology should feel warm, not cold. We write like a trusted colleague, not a vendor. We care about the people behind the clubs, and that shows in how we communicate -- with warmth, respect, and a genuine desire to help.

## 3. Our Personality

Think of GolfNext as the experienced, innovative golf pro. The one who's played thousands of rounds, understands the game deeply, and has a quiet confidence about what works. This person doesn't need to shout to be heard. They earn attention through expertise, approachability, and a genuine love for the game.

**We are:**
- Informal and approachable -- we talk with clubs, not at them.
- Visionary in ambition -- we're shaping the future of golf technology.
- Grounded in practice -- every idea is rooted in real club needs.
- Action-oriented -- we fix things fast and don't waste words.
- Quietly confident -- we know our worth without being arrogant.

**We are not:**
- Salesy or pushy -- we never pressure anyone into anything.
- Boring or corporate -- no stiff, lifeless language.
- Aggressive toward competitors -- we acknowledge them, focus on our strengths.
- Jargon-heavy -- we keep language simple enough for everyone.

## 4. How We Write

### 4.1 Tone Spectrum

GolfNext's tone slides along a spectrum depending on the context, but it always stays within our personality. Think of it as a volume dial -- the core sound stays the same, but the energy level adjusts.

**Everyday communication** (emails, support, customer conversations):
Warm, informal, direct. Like a knowledgeable colleague who genuinely wants to help. Use "you" and "your club." Keep it personal.

**Social media -- LinkedIn** (primary channel):
Inspirational and slightly more energetic. Show what's possible. Lead with success stories and club transformations. Keep the warmth, add a spark.

**Strategic content** (investor materials, core narrative, conference presentations):
Polished and visionary. More structured language, bigger-picture thinking. Still authentic -- never cold or corporate.

**Product communication** (feature announcements, updates):
Direct and benefit-led. Always answer: "What does this do for your club?" Never lead with specs or features.

### 4.2 Sentence Style

Short. Punchy. To the point. Every sentence earns its place. We don't pad our writing with filler words or unnecessary qualifiers. If a sentence doesn't add value, cut it.

That said, we're not robotic. A well-placed longer sentence can add rhythm and depth. The key is intentionality -- no word should be there by accident.

### 4.3 Addressing the Reader

| Context | How to address |
|---------|----------------|
| Individual | "you" -- always informal, never "De" (Danish formal) |
| The club | "your club" -- personal but respectful of the institution |
| GolfNext | "we" in personal contexts, "GolfNext" when the brand needs to stand on its own |

### 4.4 Language & Accessibility

We use one consistent language level across all customer communication -- whether we're talking to a tech-savvy club manager or a volunteer board member with no technical background. This means: plain language, no jargon, no acronyms without explanation. If your grandmother wouldn't understand it, rewrite it.

Our primary language is American English for international markets, and local Scandinavian languages for Denmark, Norway, and Sweden. As we expand, tone may be culturally adapted -- but these guidelines remain the foundation.

## 5. Content Principles

### 5.1 Lead with Value, Not Features

Always start with what our product does for the club -- not how it works. The club manager doesn't care about the technical architecture. They care about saving time, making money, and keeping golfers happy.

**Do this:**
- "Your players can now pay on the range faster than ever -- here's how."
- "Clubs using our kiosks report 20 fewer staff hours per month."
- "Switch software without replacing hardware. Your choice, always."

**Not this:**
- "We've updated the API endpoint for our payment module."
- "Our kiosk features a 15-inch touchscreen with IP65 rating."
- "Our proprietary infrastructure layer enables modular integration."

### 5.2 Tell Real Stories

We use real club examples whenever possible. A great GolfNext story includes three elements: the numbers (what changed), the club's own voice (a quote from the manager or owner), and the transformation (before vs. after). This combination is what makes our communication credible and relatable.

### 5.3 Humor: Understated, Never Forced

We welcome a wink, a subtle golf reference, or a dry observation. But we never force a joke. If it doesn't land naturally, leave it out. Think of the humor of a seasoned pro who's seen it all -- dry, warm, and delivered with a straight face.

**Do this:**
- "500 million balls dispensed. That's a lot of slices."
- A subtle nod to shared golf experiences
- Self-aware, light-hearted observations

**Not this:**
- Puns in headlines just because
- Memes or trendy internet humor
- Making fun of clubs, players, or competitors

## 6. Talking About Competitors

We're not afraid to name competitors when they're in play. But we do it with class. We acknowledge them, then focus on our strengths -- especially openness, freedom of choice, and our deep understanding of golf. We never trash-talk. We never create fear. We simply make it clear why GolfNext is the better partner.

**Do this:**
- "Unlike closed systems, GolfNext lets you switch software without replacing hardware."
- "We know [competitor] well. Here's what makes us different."
- Factual, confident comparisons

**Not this:**
- "[Competitor] locks you in and overcharges you."
- "Their technology is outdated and unreliable."
- Fear-based or aggressive messaging

## 7. Channel Guidelines

**Email:** Personal, direct, and warm. Write like you're emailing a colleague in the golf industry. Get to the point quickly. Always be clear about next steps or what you need from the reader.

**LinkedIn:** Our most public-facing channel. Slightly more energetic and inspirational. Lead with success stories, transformation visuals (before/after), and club testimonials. Show happy players using our products. Share results. Use video to demonstrate how easy and fast our solutions work.

**Phone & Conferences:** The same warmth and directness as in writing, but with more room for natural conversation. Listen first. When presenting, be visionary about where golf is headed -- but always ground it in practical value for the club.

## 8. When Things Go Wrong

When a club has a problem, we respond with action -- not lengthy apologies. We acknowledge, we fix, we follow up. Speed and ownership are our trademarks in these moments. The tone stays warm but becomes more focused and decisive.

**Do this:**
- "We're on it. Here's what we're doing right now."
- "Fixed. Let us know if you see anything else."
- "We take this seriously -- here's what happened and what we've changed."

**Not this:**
- "We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused."
- "We'll look into this and get back to you." (vague)
- Long, defensive explanations

## 9. Visual Communication Priorities

When choosing imagery and video for our communication, follow this priority order:

1. **People first.** Happy golfers using our products in real club settings. The human experience is always the hero.
2. **Results second.** Data, dashboards, and metrics that prove the value. Show the impact.
3. **Product demos third.** Video content showing how fast and easy our solutions work. Let people see simplicity in action.

## 10. The One Rule

> "Know your stuff. Write with warmth."

If every piece of GolfNext communication follows this principle, we'll always sound like who we are: golfers building technology for golfers. Credible, knowledgeable, and deeply human.

**GolfNext -- The Future in Play.**
